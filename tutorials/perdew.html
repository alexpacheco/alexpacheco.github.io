Conversion Factors


1 hartree = 27.21 eV = 627.5 kcal/mol = 219475 cm-1,

1 bohr = 0.5292 Å.

Ionization Potential


I = E(singly positive atomic ion) - E(neutral atom).

Atomization Energy of molecule


AE(static) = E(separate atoms) - E (non-vibrating molecule).

Zero-Point Energy


hbar*omega/2 = hbar*sqrt(k/mu)/2, where k is the spring constant and mu = M1*M2/(M1+M2) is the reduced mass.

Atomization Energy of diatomic molecule


AE = E(separate atoms) - E(vibrating molecule)
       = AE(static) - hbar*omega/2.

Experimental values from the Computational Chemistry Comparison and Benchmark Database (cccbd.nist.gov), for nitrogen:

AE(N2) = 225.06 kcal/mol

  • The static-nucleus equilibrium d for N2, from the zero of the Hellmann-Feynman force on each nucleus
  • The vibrational zero-point energy of N2 at equilibrium AE(N2).
  • All species should be in their ground states.
